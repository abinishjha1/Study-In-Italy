Study in Italy Guide

Step-by-step articles to plan your move.

How to Apply to Italian Universities

  1. Choose programs and check deadlines
  2. Prepare documents (SOP, LoRs, CV, transcripts)
  3. Language certificates (IELTS/TOEFL or Italian)
  4. Submit online applications
  5. Apply for scholarships
  6. Receive admission and pay fees

Living Costs in Italy

  • Accommodation: €300–€700/month (city dependent)
  • Food & groceries: €150–€250/month
  • Transport: €25–€35/month student pass
  • Health insurance: €150–€200/year

Visa Process for Students

Gather acceptance letter, financial proof, housing, insurance, and schedule a consulate appointment. Allow 4–8 weeks for processing.

Tips for International Students

  • Start applications 6–10 months early
  • Prepare translations and apostille in advance
  • Practice basic Italian for daily life
  • Keep copies of all documents
